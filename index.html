Born in Ohio, USA, to a Lebanese father and a Syrian mother, Mayar completed his secondary education in Paris, France. In 2018, he enrolled at École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) as an environmental sciences and engineering student. He is now an MEng candidate in civil and environmental engineering at Imperial College London.

Throughout his studies, Mayar conducted research on econometric modelling of travel demand, and network performance and equilibrium. He also gained expertise in nonlinear structural analysis and computational engineering analysis.

In 2023, Mayar joined MIT as a visiting student to undertake research at Senseable City Lab. His research currently involves developing optimisation models to maximise the monitoring of urban environments through drive-by sensing.

Mayar is a student member at IET, ICE, and a permanent CEM YPN member of IUCN, where he is part of the Urban and Mediterranean-type Ecosystems task forces.
